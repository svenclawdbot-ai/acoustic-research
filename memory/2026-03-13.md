**Context:** Acoustic NDE / Medical Elastography Research (Week 2)

### Challenge
Given the 1D FDTD shear wave simulator you built, derive the **complex wave number** \( k^* \) for a Kelvin-Voigt viscoelastic material:

\[
k^* = \omega \sqrt{\frac{\rho}{\mu + i\omega\eta}}
\]

Where:
- \(\mu\) = shear modulus (Pa)
- \(\eta\) = viscosity (Pa·s)
- \(\rho\) = density (kg/m³)
- \(\omega\) = angular frequency (rad/s)

**Tasks:**
1. Separate \( k^* = k' - i k'' \) into real (propagation) and imaginary (attenuation) parts
2. Derive the phase velocity \( c_p(\omega) = \omega / k' \) — show it increases with frequency (dispersion)
3. Calculate the attenuation coefficient \(\alpha = k''\) and its frequency dependence
4. **Extension:** Implement frequency-dependent phase velocity in your FDTD simulator and compare wave packets at 50Hz vs 200Hz

**Deliverable:** Mathematical derivation + code modification + visualization comparison
