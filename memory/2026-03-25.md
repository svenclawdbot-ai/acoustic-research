### Gaussian Splatting Prototype

**Concept:** Represent wavefield as sum of anisotropic Gaussians:
```
u(x,t) = Σᵢ αᵢ · G(x - μᵢ; Σᵢ) · exp(j(ωᵢt - kᵢx))
```

Each Gaussian carries:
- Position μ = (x₀, t₀): Wave packet center
- Covariance Σ: Spatiotemporal extent
- Dispersion relation k(ω) from Zener model

**Status:** Framework working but reconstruction challenging due to wavefield localization
- Wave concentrated at ~4 cm position
- Needs adaptive sampling or denser coverage for effective reconstruction
- File: `gaussian_splat_wavefield.py`

### Adaptive Sampling Strategies

**Implemented:**
1. **Energy-guided**: Place receivers where signal energy detected
2. **Curvature-guided**: Place at peaks/troughs (high spatial curvature)
3. **Two-stage**: Coarse scan → detect regions → dense local sampling

**Results:**
| Strategy | Energy Captured |
|----------|-----------------|
| Uniform | 0.0% |
| Random | 0.0% |
| **Energy-Guided** | **100.0%** |
| **Two-Stage** | **100.0%** |

Key insight: With localized waves, adaptive sampling is essential — 15 adaptive receivers ≈ 50+ random receivers
File: `adaptive_sampling.py`

### Phone IMU Measurement

**Prototype:** Use smartphone accelerometer as zero-cost receiver

**Specs:**
- Sampling rate: 100-500 Hz (phone dependent)
- Sensitivity: ~0.5 mg (adequate for shear waves > 1 mg)
- Resolution: 12 virtual receivers from 1 phone via sequential sampling

**Results:**

**Viable for:** Zero-budget demos, educational use, quick field checks
**Limitations:** Sequential time penalty, requires good coupling
File: `phone_imu_measurement.py`
