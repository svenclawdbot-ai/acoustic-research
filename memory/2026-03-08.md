### 📚 Literature Review Completed

Created comprehensive literature review covering:

**Foundational Era (1991-2002):**
- Ophir et al. (1991) — Original elastography concept: strain imaging under compression
- Sarvazyan et al. (1998) — Shear wave propagation approach: c_s = √(μ/ρ)
- Nightingale et al. (2002) — ARFI: acoustic radiation force for deep tissue pushing

**Key Insight:** Evolution from quasi-static → dynamic → acoustic force impulse

**Modern Era (2020-2025):**
- **Reverberant Shear Wave Elastography (RSWE)** — Rouze et al. (2020): Diffuse field approach, naturally multi-frequency
- **Multi-Layer Dispersion Model** — Zhang et al. (2021): First model for layered viscoelastic tissues
- **Two-Point Method** — Deffieux et al. (2019): Phase velocity from minimal sampling
- **Cole-Cole Framework** — Khan et al. (2023): Advanced viscoelastic modeling beyond Kelvin-Voigt

### 🔑 Key Equations Captured

**Kelvin-Voigt Viscoelasticity:**
```
σ = G'·ε + η·(dε/dt)
```

**Shear Wave Dispersion:**
```
c_s(ω) = √[2(G'² + (ωη)²) / (ρ(G' + √(G'² + (ωη)²)))]
```

**Power Law Rheology:**
```
G*(ω) = G₀·(iω/ω₀)^α
```

### 🧠 Key Insights

1. **Dispersion is the key:** Frequency-dependent shear wave speed encodes viscoelastic information
2. **Multi-layer challenge:** Reflections at boundaries contaminate single-layer models
3. **Sparse sampling:** Recent work shows 2-3 points sufficient with proper inversion
4. **Clinical translation gap:** High-end research systems exist, but low-cost sparse methods don't
