### The Nucleo Problem
The Nucleo-H743ZI2 is the designated "measurement brain" (ADC + DSP). It's missing from the order. Without it, the dual-processor architecture (Nucleo measures, ESP32 communicates) is blocked.

### 💡 Proposed Solution: Single-Board Prototype on ESP32-S3
The ESP32-S3 has a 2-channel 12-bit SAR ADC (up to 2 Msps with DMA). For the prototype, we can run the ENTIRE measurement chain on the ESP32-S3:

- **SPI** → AD9833 (sweep 1 kHz → 1 MHz)
- **ADC** → TIA output (GPIO pin)
- **Software lock-in** → Quadrature demodulation on ESP32-S3
- **WiFi/BLE** → Built into ESP32-S3 (no LoRa needed for bench test)

**Advantages:**
- Unblocks prototype completion TODAY
- No waiting for Nucleo reorder (2-5 days)
- Simpler wiring (single board)
- Full C++ lock-in implementation tested before porting to Nucleo

**Limitations:**
- ESP32-S3 ADC not as fast/precise as Nucleo (12-bit vs 16-bit, lower SNR)
- Single-core lock-in may limit max frequency or sample count
- Field deployment still wants Nucleo + LoRa for robustness

**This is a PROTOTYPE — proving the physics works.** Precision comes later.
