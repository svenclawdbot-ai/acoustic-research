### Background

You've mastered single-element ultrasonic acquisition. Today you level up to **phased array ultrasonics (PAUT)** — the industry standard for advanced NDE. By controlling the timing (phase) of emissions across multiple elements, you can:

## Part 5: Resolution Analysis & Optimization (30 min)

### Problem
Analyze and optimize your beamforming performance.

### Tasks

1. **Calculate theoretical resolution**
   Lateral resolution at focus:
   ```
   Δx ≈ λ · z_f / (n_elements · p) · (1/cos(θ))
   ```
   
   Axial resolution:
   ```
   Δz ≈ c / (2 · bandwidth)
   ```

2. **Measure actual beam profile**
   From your simulation, extract:
   - Main lobe width (-6dB)
   - Peak side lobe level
   - Grating lobe locations (if any)

3. **Optimize apodization**
   Test window functions:
   - Rectangular (uniform)
   - Hamming
   - Hanning
   - Blackman

   Compare side lobe suppression vs main lobe widening.

## Key Equations Reference

| Parameter | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| Steering delay | $\Delta t_n = n \cdot p \cdot \sin(\theta) / c$ |
| Focal delay | $t_n = (\sqrt{z_f^2 + (x_n - x_f)^2} - z_f) / c$ |
| Lateral resolution | $\Delta x = \lambda \cdot F\# = \lambda \cdot z_f / D$ |
| F-number | $F\# = z_f / (n_{active} \cdot p)$ |
| Grating lobe angle | $\sin(\theta_{gl}) = \sin(\theta) \pm m \cdot \lambda / p$ |
