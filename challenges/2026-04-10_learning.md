## Part 3: Case Study — Real-World Dilemma (45 min)

### Scenario: The Incidental Finding

You're testing your real-time shear wave system at a partner hospital. During a routine liver stiffness measurement on a 45-year-old asymptomatic patient, your high-resolution mode accidentally captures adjacent cardiac motion. The AI flags what might be an early-stage atrial septal defect — a congenital heart condition the patient never knew about.

**Complications:**
- This was NOT the purpose of the scan (liver screening)
- The finding is uncertain (AI confidence: 67%)
- The patient has a high-stress job (airline pilot)
- Reporting it could trigger FAA medical review and career consequences
- Not reporting it violates "duty to warn" principles
- The system was never validated for cardiac imaging

### Your Tasks

1. **Stakeholder Mapping**
   List all stakeholders and their interests:
   - Patient (autonomy, health, career)
   - Clinician (duty of care, liability)
   - You as developer (scope of use, safety)
   - Hospital (risk management)
   - Society (safety vs. privacy)

2. **Ethical Framework Application**
   Apply two frameworks:
   - **Consequentialist:** What actions produce the best overall outcome?
   - **Deontological:** What duties and rules apply regardless of outcome?

3. **Design Response**
   How would you modify your system to prevent or better handle such situations?
   - Technical solutions (spatial masking, indication-specific training)
   - Protocol solutions (consent forms, radiologist review workflows)
   - Policy solutions (scope of practice, incident reporting)

## Part 4: Philosophical Deep Dive — Taleb's Antifragility (45 min)

### Read
- **Nassim Nicholas Taleb — *Antifragile* (Chapter: The Antifragility of the Technological)**
- Or: [Farnam Street summary of Antifragility](https://fs.blog)

### Core Concepts

| Concept | Definition | Application to Your Work |
|---------|-----------|-------------------------|
| Fragile | Breaks under stress | Rigid diagnostic criteria that fail on edge cases |
| Robust | Resists stress | Conservative imaging protocols with wide safety margins |
| Antifragile | Gains from stress | Systems that learn from errors and adapt |

### Challenge Questions

1. **Is your phased array system fragile, robust, or antifragile?**
   - What stresses could break it?
   - How could you design it to improve with use?

2. **The "Lindy Effect" in Medical Technology**
   Technologies that have survived longest are likely to survive longest.
   - Why has basic ultrasound survived since the 1940s despite newer modalities?
   - What does this tell you about over-engineering vs. fundamental principles?

3. **Via Negativa in Engineering**
   "Subtraction is better than addition."
   - What features could you REMOVE from your system to make it more reliable?
   - How does complexity increase fragility in diagnostic systems?

### Written Response (400-600 words)
**"How would you redesign your acoustic NDE system to be antifragile?"**
