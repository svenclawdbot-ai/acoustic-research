# Thursday Learning Challenge — May 7, 2026
## Topic: Technical Communication — The Investor Pitch Refinement

### Context
You just committed the TurboQuant V5 investor pitch deck. Today, refine your ability to communicate complex engineering to non-technical audiences.

### The Challenge

**Part 1: Elevator Pitch (30 min)**
Draft a 60-second verbal pitch for TurboQuant V5 that:
- Opens with the problem (why current NDE is inadequate)
- States the solution in one sentence (no jargon)
- Quantifies the opportunity (£TAM, customer pain)
- Ends with what you need (£X to reach milestone Y)

Constraint: No acronyms without instant expansion. No "leveraging synergistic paradigms."

**Part 2: Visual Abstract (45 min)**
Create a one-page visual abstract for a paper titled:
"Ultrasonic Shear Wave Elastography for Non-Destructive Evaluation of Carbon Fiber Composites"

Requirements:
- One compelling figure (mockup or hand sketch)
- 150-word summary in plain English
- Three bullet takeaways for engineers
- One sentence on why a layperson should care

**Part 3: Peer Review Practice (45 min)**
Read the abstract of a real paper (I’ll provide one). Write a 200-word constructive peer review that:
- Identifies one genuine strength
- Raises one methodological concern politely
- Suggests one concrete improvement
- Maintains collegial tone throughout

### Deliverables
1. Recorded or written 60-second elevator pitch
2. Visual abstract (ASCII art, sketch, or digital mockup)
3. Peer review critique with tone checklist verified

### Success Criteria
- A non-engineer can understand the pitch in one listen
- The visual abstract communicates without reading the text
- The peer review is something you'd be happy to receive yourself

### Resources
- "The Craft of Scientific Writing" — Michael Alley
- TED Talk analysis: How to pitch a complex idea (pick any)
- Your own V5 pitch deck for reference material

*Focus: Communication is a force multiplier for good engineering*
