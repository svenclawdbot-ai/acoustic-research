## 🎯 The Limitations of Kelvin-Voigt: When Does It Break?

### Context
Your research has operated primarily within the **Zener model** (standard linear solid), but many ultrasound elastography papers still use the simpler **Kelvin-Voigt** model. These models predict different wave dispersion behavior. Understanding where they diverge is essential for interpreting literature and justifying your modeling choices.

---

### Part 1: Derive the Dispersion Relations (60 min)

Derive the complex wavenumber $k(\omega)$ for shear waves in both models from first principles.

**Kelvin-Voigt:**  
$G^*(\omega) = G' + i\omega\eta$  
Find $c_p(\omega)$ and $\alpha(\omega)$

**Zener (Standard Linear Solid):**  
$G^*(\omega) = G_\infty - \frac{G_\infty - G_0}{1 + i\omega\tau_\sigma}$  
Find $c_p(\omega)$ and $\alpha(\omega)$

**Questions to answer:**
- At what limits ($\omega \to 0$, $\omega \to \infty$) do both models agree?
- What does Kelvin-Voigt predict for phase velocity at *high* frequencies? Is this physically realistic?
- What parameter in the Zener model controls the transition frequency?

### Part 2: Numerical Comparison (45 min)

Now plot over the range $f = 0.1$ Hz to $10{,}000$ Hz:

**Questions to answer:**

What is the likely direction and magnitude of the error if the radiologist assumes this number is frequency-independent?*
